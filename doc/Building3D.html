Building 3D Printed Walking Robot Chassis

This drawing will help explain my conventions for naming parts of the linkage:

BoM

For a complete chassis hardware setup, using 3/16" OD nylon spacers you will want :

  • (2) gearhead motors
  • L298N motor driver module
  • (2) 3/16" OD brass tubes or steel rods, 1 foot long, or similar sized kebab skewers
  • CA cement, gap filling, medium-viscosity, and/or
    JB weld, or similar epoxy good for bonding plastic to metal
  • (8) 1/2" long, 3/16" OD spacers for #4 screws
  • (24) 3/16" long, 3/16" OD spacers for #4 screws
  • (8) 1/2" long, 3/16" OD standoffs for #4-40 screws
  • (2) #4-40 0.25 to 0.75 inch threaded rods.
    Can be made by cutting the head off of a screw, and then cleaning the threads.
  • (24) #4 flat washers, optional, for leg joints.
    Might be good for reliability of leg joints, but not absolutely necessary
  • (4) #4 larger diameter flat washers, if available. (standard diameter is OK)
    for crank spindles.
  • (4) #4 internal tooth lock washers
    for spindles
  • (36-48) 3/16+" ID nylon washers.
    can vary for each build. They can be used as shims to make parts align and rotate better
  • (24) #4-40 1/2" screws
  • (8) #4-40 1" screws
  • (32) #4-40 nyloc nuts
  • 2 Scunchii brand silicone hair ties (drive belts)
  • small cable ties, 8-16 or so.
  • (8-14) #4ish plastic screws.
    They are optional to attach the payload platforms. You can use either screws (and more washers), or cable ties

Electronics

For the optional baseline remote-control-over-bluetooth electronics, you will need:
  • Arduino Nano
  • Arduino Nano breakout board
  • HC-06 or similar serial-over-bluetooth module
  • 3-cell battery holder
  • wires, solder, soldering iron
  • firmware for Arduino
  • Remote control app for Android
  • 14500 LiIon batteries.
    6 are recommended so that you can alternate batteries from the charger to the ones in-use.
  • LiIon battery charger

Drills

After 3D printing the linkage, holes may need to be precision machined and/or polished.
BitNotes
#10 free on 3/16" OD axle and standoffs
#11 [optional. #10 should work]
free on 3/16" OD axle and standoffs, if "polished" after drill
#12 Snug (but not press-fit) on 3/16" OD, good for non-moving connections to B-axle
#35 snug, but free around #4-40 screw

Models to print

One can print all parts before starting assembly. However, in the assembly instructions we recommend which parts to print just-in-time. When the chassis is assembled in this manner, if there is a problem with a part, it can be re-printed as needed. For most consumer grade 3D printers, assembling the chassis takes a similar amount of time as printing the chassis, so both tasks can be performed in parallel.

Printed parts list:

  • (4) mainBar.stl
  • (2) mainPulley.stl
  • (4) AC.stl (crank arm)
  • (2) motorMount.stl
  • (4) hip.stl
  • (4) mirror image of hip.stl
  • (4) foot.stl
  • (4) mirror image of foot.stl
  • (8) CH.stl (lower crank link)
  • (8) CD.stl (upper crank link)
  • (16) EF.stl (hip to knee link)
  • (16) BH.stl (axle to hamstring link)
  • (2) motorPulley.stl
  • (2) payloadBrace.stl
  • (1) payloadPlatform.stl
  • (1) payloadPlatformLower.stl
  • [optional] payloadLid.stl
  • (2) brace.stl (outer brace)
  • (4) clip.stl (retension clips)
A notch can be filed into the axles to place the retension clips.
A simple rubber band on the axle will work if you do not wish to notch the axles.

Proceed to Assembly instructions.

Instructions will be included for the baseline remote-control-over-bluetooth electronics. Users are encouraged to replace the Arduino Nano master controller with a microcontroller and/or software of their choice. The L298N motor driver modules are very common, and many libraries exist to assist custom robot builders in their use. Source code for our basic remote control app includes our custom L298N driver library.
